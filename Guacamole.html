Guacamole

Description

Guacamole is a creamy and flavorful Mexican dip or spread made primarily from ripe avocados. It has a rich texture with a balance of tangy and savory flavors, often enhanced with ingredients like lime juice, cilantro, tomatoes, onions, and various seasonings.

Ingredients

  • 3 ripe avocados

  • 1 lime (juiced)

  • 1/2 teaspoon salt (or to taste)

  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin

  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)

  • 1/2 medium red onion, diced

  • 2 Roma tomatoes, diced (seeds removed)

  • 1-2 tablespoons fresh cilantro, chopped

  • 1 clove garlic, minced

Instructions

  1. Prepare the avocados: Cut the avocados in half, remove the pits, and scoop the flesh into a mixing bowl.

  2. Mash the avocados: Use a fork or potato masher to mash the avocados to your desired consistency (smooth or chunky).

  3. Mix in the lime juice, salt, and spices: Add lime juice, salt, cumin, and cayenne pepper (if using) to the mashed avocados. Mix well.

  4. Add the remaining ingredients: Gently fold in the diced onion, tomatoes, cilantro, and minced garlic until evenly combined.

  5. Taste and adjust: Taste the guacamole and adjust seasoning if needed, adding more salt or lime juice to balance the flavors.

  6. Serve or store: Serve immediately with tortilla chips or as a topping for tacos, nachos, or any Mexican dish. If not serving immediately, cover tightly with plastic wrap directly on the surface of the guacamole to prevent browning. Guacamole can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 2 days.

Storage

To store guacamole, place it in an airtight container with plastic wrap pressed directly onto the surface of the guacamole to minimize exposure to air.

This helps prevent oxidation and keeps it from turning brown. Stored this way, guacamole can last in the refrigerator for up to 2 days, though it's best served fresh.
