Spaghetti Carbonara

Description

Spaghetti Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish known for its creamy sauce made from eggs, cheese, and cured pork (typically pancetta or guanciale).

It's a simple yet flavorful dish that's loved for its rich, silky texture and savory flavors.

Ingredients

  • 350g spaghetti

  • 150g pancetta or guanciale, diced (you can also use bacon as a substitute)

  • 3 large eggs

  • 50g grated Pecorino Romano cheese (or Parmesan cheese), plus extra for serving

  • Freshly ground black pepper

  • Salt (if needed, as the pancetta or guanciale and cheese are already salty)

  • Chopped fresh parsley (optional, for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Cook the spaghetti: In a large pot of boiling salted water, cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions until al dente. Reserve about 1 cup of pasta cooking water before draining.

  2. Prepare the sauce: While the spaghetti is cooking, whisk together the eggs, grated cheese, and a generous amount of freshly ground black pepper in a bowl. Set aside.

  3. Cook the pancetta/guanciale: In a large skillet or frying pan, cook the diced pancetta or guanciale over medium heat until crispy and browned. Remove from heat and set aside.

  4. Combine pasta and sauce: Immediately after draining the spaghetti, return it to the pot or a large bowl. Add the cooked pancetta/guanciale and toss to combine. Quickly pour the egg and cheese mixture over the hot pasta, tossing continuously until the eggs coat the spaghetti and create a creamy sauce. If the sauce is too thick, add some reserved pasta cooking water gradually until it reaches a creamy consistency.

  5. Serve: Divide the spaghetti carbonara among serving plates. Garnish with additional grated cheese, freshly ground black pepper, and chopped parsley (if using).

Storage

Spaghetti Carbonara is best enjoyed immediately after cooking while the sauce is creamy and silky.

Leftovers can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 days. Reheat gently in a skillet over low heat, adding a splash of cream or pasta cooking water to revive the creamy texture.

It's not recommended to freeze Spaghetti Carbonara as the texture of the eggs may change upon thawing.
